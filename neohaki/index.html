Nor does all matter interact via the electromagnetic spectrum: neutral particles, gravity, and the strong and weak nuclear forces operate outside it, so electromagnetism is one of several fundamental interactions, not the one that shapes the entire universe. Regarding Rayleigh scattering, it is not the spectrum giving shape to matter but the reverse: particles much smaller than the wavelength of light scatter blue light more than red, with intensity proportional to one over lambda to the fourth, and that is why I see the sky as blue; it is interaction, not origin. My memory of Rayleigh in Sabaody is accurate in essence, since Silvers Rayleigh teaches Luffy Haki there, with the tension of Admiral Kizaru in the background. And my intuition that Armament Haki exists makes sense not as literal physics — that does not occur — but as conceptual isomorphism… can an observer's will cross a threshold and become operationally real within a system?"
